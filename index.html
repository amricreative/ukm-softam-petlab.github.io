Introduction to PET Lab

Emerging technologies refer to new innovative technologies that are currently being developed or are in the early stages of adoption, that have the potential to disrupt existing industries and create new markets. Some examples of emerging technologies include artificial intelligence, blockchain, virtual and augmented reality, 5G, quantum computing, biotechnology, and renewable energy. One of the key drivers of emerging technologies is the need to address complex challenges in areas such as healthcare, energy, and the environment.


Meanwhile, Platform technology refers to a type of technology that allows multiple applications and services to be built on top of a common foundation. This technology enables developers to create new products and services faster and more efficiently by leveraging existing infrastructure and tools. Platform technology is rapidly becoming the backbone of the digital economy that helps revolutionizing the way we interact with each other and do business online, creating new features and functionality, and enabling new forms of communication and collaboration.


At PET Lab, we focus on both the Platform and the Emerging Technology, whether its either one of them or a combination of both. The purpose of our lab is to help design the foundation for running usable applications for business, government and/or community; by enabling new and disruptive technologies research with the goal of understanding their impact on our next generation network, mobile and/or other immersive technologies;
